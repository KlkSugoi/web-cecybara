Relative Clauses

What is the Relative Clauses?

A relative clause is a type of subordinate clause that complements a noun. For example: the man [who was not here] he [to whom I had written] the [that I told you yesterday] The sentence contains a name man whose reference is specified or limited by the sentence that was not here.

We use relative clauses to give additional information about something without starting another sentence. By combining sentences with a relative clause, your text becomes more fluent and you can avoid repeating certain words.
