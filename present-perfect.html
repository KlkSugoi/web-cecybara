What is the Present Perfect?

The "present perfect" is used to indicate a link between the present and the past. The time in which the action takes place is prior to the present but is unspecified and, often, a greater interest falls on the result than on the action itself.

Present Perfect Simple is the past perfect of English. It allows connecting the past with the present: a completed past action gives rise to a state or situation that continues to be valid in the present. It is formed with the present of the auxiliary verb have and the participle of the main verb.
